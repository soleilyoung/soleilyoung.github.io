Research Interests

I am a mycologist and evolutionary biologist interested in the evolution and maintance of sexual reproduction, genomic organization, and how symbiotic interactions like mutualism and parasitism affect genetic variation. I am particularly intersted in studying these phenomenon in basidiomycete fungi, which spend a long portion of their life as dikaryons, where each cell contains two (often) genetically distinct haploid nuclei. This life history trait allows us to ask unique questions about cell idenity, genomic conflict, and ploidy such as:

  • Are dikaryotic cells equivalent to diploid cells?
  • How is transcription coordinated between nuclei in a cell? Are some nuclei silent?
  • What are the evolutionary pressures that drive the seperation of plasmogamy (cell fusion) and karyogamy (nuclear fusion)?
